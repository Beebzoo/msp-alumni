Privacy Policy

How we handle data on the MSP Alumni Network website

Overview

The MSP Alumni Network website is operated by the Maastricht Science Programme at the Faculty of Science and Engineering, Maastricht University. We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling data responsibly.

This privacy policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your data.

What This Website Displays

This website showcases aggregate, anonymized statistics about MSP alumni outcomes. The data displayed includes:

  • Total number of alumni by graduation year
  • Geographic distribution by country (counts, not individuals)
  • Master's programme destinations by university (counts, not individuals)
  • Career sector breakdown (aggregate percentages)
  • PhD and academic pathway statistics

Privacy by Design

No individual alumni names, contact details, or personal information is displayed on this website without explicit consent. All statistics are aggregated to protect individual privacy.

Data Collection on This Website

This website is a static informational site. We collect minimal data:

  • No cookies are used for tracking purposes
  • No personal data is collected through this website
  • No user accounts or login is required
  • Basic, privacy-friendly analytics may be used to understand page views (no personal identifiers collected)

Alumni Data Management

The MSP Alumni Network maintains a separate internal database for alumni engagement purposes. This database:

  • Contains contact information provided voluntarily by alumni
  • Is used for newsletters, event invitations, and community building
  • Is managed in accordance with GDPR requirements
  • Is not publicly accessible through this website

If you are an MSP alumnus and want to know what data we hold about you, update your information, or request deletion, please contact us using the details below.

Success Stories

Alumni success stories featured on this website or in our materials are published only with explicit, informed consent from the individual alumnus. If you have been featured and wish to modify or remove your story, please contact us.

Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access - Request a copy of data we hold about you
  • Rectification - Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure - Request deletion of your data
  • Restriction - Limit how we process your data
  • Objection - Object to processing based on legitimate interest
  • Portability - Receive your data in a structured format

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the email address below.
