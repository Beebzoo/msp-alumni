Why MSP

A Unique Approach to Science Education

MSP combines the flexibility of a Liberal Arts & Sciences education with rigorous natural science training

Design Your Curriculum

Choose from 150+ courses across biology, chemistry, physics, mathematics, and interdisciplinary fields. Your academic advisor helps you build a path to your dream master's programme.

Complete academic freedom

Problem-Based Learning

Learn in small groups of 12-15 students. Tackle real scientific challenges together. Develop critical thinking, communication, and collaboration skills that set you apart.

Active, engaging education

Research-Based Learning

Conduct original research from year one. Work on projects with unknown answers. Present your findings at student conferences. Build your scientific portfolio early.

Real research experience

State-of-the-Art Labs

Access cutting-edge facilities at Dubois Domein and the Brightlands Chemelot Campus. Work with advanced equipment used by industry professionals.

Industry-grade facilities

International Community

Join 60+ nationalities in one programme. Experience diverse perspectives in every discussion. Build a global network that lasts a lifetime.

Truly global perspective

Honours Programme

Challenge yourself further with the Honours Research Programme. Start real research from day one. Work with top scientists. Get a head start on your academic career.

For ambitious students
Research Ecosystem

World-Class Research Partners

MSP students gain access to cutting-edge research facilities and industry collaborations

Brightlands Chemelot

Europe's leading chemistry and materials campus. Home to 50+ companies and 8,000 professionals innovating in sustainable materials.

M4I Institute

Maastricht MultiModal Molecular Imaging Institute. Pioneering mass spectrometry imaging for biomedical research.

Nikhef

National Institute for Subatomic Physics. Partner in major international particle physics experiments including CERN.

AMIBM

Aachen-Maastricht Institute for Biobased Materials. Developing the sustainable materials of tomorrow.

Coming to Limburg

The Einstein Telescope

The next generation of gravitational wave detection is coming to your backyard. The Einstein Telescope a 2 billion underground observatory is planned for the Limburg-Belgium-Germany border region. As an MSP student, you'll be at the forefront of this scientific revolution.
