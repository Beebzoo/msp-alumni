Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2025

Introduction

The Maastricht Science Programme (MSP) values your privacy. This policy explains how we handle personal data for the MSP Alumni Network — a community platform that helps us stay connected with you and showcase what our graduates achieve together.

By keeping track of where our alumni are and what paths they've taken, we can maintain an up-to-date picture of our global community — from the countries you live in, to the universities you've attended, to the careers you've built. This information helps current students see what's possible after MSP, and helps you connect with fellow alumni around the world.

We are committed to processing your data fairly, transparently, and in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The MSP Alumni Network operates as part of Maastricht University's alumni engagement activities. For comprehensive information about how Maastricht University handles personal data, please refer to the UM General Privacy Statement and the UM Privacy Statements.

What Data We Process

We process a limited set of personal data to maintain our alumni community and keep our records accurate. This allows us to show where MSP alumni are in the world, which universities they've gone on to, and what fields they work in — creating a living picture of our network that benefits everyone.

Data from university records

When you graduate, we retain basic information from your student record:

  • Name and contact details (email address, phone number)
  • Date of birth and nationality
  • Programme and graduation date

Data you provide

Through our graduation survey or when you update us later, you may share:

  • Your current location (city/country)
  • Your Master's programme and university
  • Your current role and employer
  • Communication preferences

This information helps us keep our community map and university destination overviews accurate — so when a prospective student asks "where do MSP graduates go?", we can give them a truthful, up-to-date answer.

Publicly available information

To keep our records accurate and our overviews current, we may occasionally check publicly available information on professional networking sites such as LinkedIn. This helps us verify details — for example, confirming which Master's programme you completed or which city you're now based in — so that our alumni statistics remain reliable.

Please note: We only look at information you have chosen to make public. We never attempt to access private profiles. If you would prefer we don't use this source for your records, simply let us know and we will rely solely on information you provide directly.

Why We Process Your Data

We use alumni data to build and maintain a community that benefits both you and future MSP students:

Purpose How this helps
Staying in touch Keeping you informed about MSP news, events, and opportunities to connect with fellow alumni
Mapping our community Maintaining accurate overviews of where alumni live, which universities they've attended, and what careers they've pursued — so you can see the full reach of our network
Helping prospective students Showing future students real outcomes (e.g., "X alumni went to ETH Zürich", "Y% work in research") so they can make informed choices
Connecting alumni Facilitating networking through country-based WhatsApp groups and helping alumni in similar fields or locations find each other — always with your consent

Legal Basis

We process your data based on:

  • Legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR): Maintaining an alumni community and tracking graduate outcomes are standard activities for a university programme. We have assessed that this processing does not override your rights, particularly because you can opt out at any time and we only use data in ways you would reasonably expect.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR): For optional activities — such as featuring your story on our website, joining WhatsApp groups, or participating in mentorship — we always ask for your explicit permission first.

What Appears on the Public Website

The MSP Alumni Network website displays aggregate statistics only — the kind of overview that helps everyone understand where our community is and what alumni have achieved. This includes:

  • Total number of alumni and their geographic distribution (e.g., "445 alumni in the Netherlands")
  • Which universities alumni have gone on to attend (e.g., "19 alumni at Imperial College London")
  • Career sectors and PhD statistics (as totals and percentages)
  • Country profiles showing cities, nationalities, and graduation year breakdowns
Your individual information is never displayed publicly without your explicit consent. The statistics you see on the website are always aggregated — we show that "8 alumni went to Cambridge", not who those individuals are. We only publish names, photos, or individual stories if you have specifically agreed to be featured.

Who We Share Data With

We do not sell your data. We may share information only with:

  • Maastricht University central services — The Alumni Office and Marketing & Communications, for coordinated university-wide alumni activities
  • Research partners — Only anonymised, aggregate data for academic research purposes
  • Service providers — Technical providers for website hosting and email services, all based within the EU

We never share your individual contact details with external parties without your permission.

How Long We Keep Your Data

Alumni relationships are lifelong, and the value of our community overview grows over time — being able to show where alumni are 5, 10, or 20 years after graduation tells a richer story than recent data alone. Therefore, we retain alumni data indefinitely unless you ask us to delete it.

If you request deletion, we will remove your personal details promptly. However, certain records must be retained for legal reasons:

Data type Retention Reason
Degree records 30 years minimum Dutch Public Records Act
Contact information Until you request deletion Ongoing relationship
Career outcomes Indefinitely (can be anonymised on request) Longitudinal community overview
Opt-out records Indefinitely To ensure we respect your preferences

Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access — Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Rectification — Ask us to correct any inaccurate information
  • Erasure — Request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Restriction — Ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Portability — Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Object — Object to processing based on legitimate interest
  • Withdraw consent — Withdraw any consent you have given, at any time

To exercise your rights, please use the GDPR request form provided by Maastricht University, or contact us directly at the address below.

Data Security

We take appropriate measures to protect your data:

  • Access to alumni records is restricted to authorised MSP staff only
  • All connections to our website use secure encryption (HTTPS)
  • Our systems are hosted within the European Union
  • We conduct regular reviews of our security practices

Cookies

This website uses only essential cookies required for basic functionality. We do not use tracking cookies, advertising cookies, or any cookies that collect personal data. We do not share browsing data with third parties.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. If we make significant changes, we will notify registered alumni by email. The "last updated" date at the top of this page shows when the policy was last revised.
