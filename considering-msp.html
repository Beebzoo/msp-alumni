What is the Maastricht Science Programme?

MSP is a 3-year English-taught Bachelor's programme where you design your own science degree. Unlike traditional programmes that lock you into one subject, MSP lets you combine courses across biology, chemistry, physics, mathematics, and neuroscience.

Research From Day One

Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from prospective students

What makes MSP different from other science programmes?

+

MSP lets you design your own curriculum by combining courses across biology, chemistry, physics, math, and neuroscience. Instead of being locked into one subject, you create a personalized degree that matches your interests and career goals.

What are my career options after MSP?

+

91% of our graduates continue to Master's programmes at 241+ universities worldwide. Alumni work in research, consulting, tech, healthcare, sustainability, and entrepreneurship from PhD candidates at MIT to founders of cleantech startups.

Is the programme taught in English?

+

Yes, MSP is fully taught in English. Our international community includes students from 87 nationalities, making it a truly global learning environment.

What is Problem-Based Learning (PBL)?

+

PBL is Maastricht University's innovative teaching method. You learn in small groups of 12-15 students, tackling real-world problems together rather than just listening to lectures. This develops critical thinking and collaboration skills.

Can I do research during my Bachelor's?

+

Absolutely! You'll have hands-on lab work throughout the programme and complete a Bachelor Thesis Research project in your final year. Past students have done research at institutions like CERN, Max Planck, and Oxford.

How do I apply?

+

Applications open in October each year through Studielink. You'll need to meet specific entry requirements including a qualifying diploma and proof of English proficiency. Visit the official UM website for detailed application information.
